## Stand‑Alone ESP32 + GPS Power Circuit

This canvas describes a simple, field‑ready power solution using a single‑cell 3.7 V Li‑ion/LiPo battery, a TP4056 charging board, a boost converter, and an ON/OFF slider switch.

### Components

* **Li‑ion/LiPo battery** (3.7 V nominal)
* **TP4056 module** with protection (IN+/IN‑, OUT+/OUT‑)
* **Boost converter** (3.7 V → 5 V, e.g. MT3608 or similar)
* **Slider switch** (SPDT or SPST, used as ON/OFF)
* **ESP32 board** (powered via 5 V pin)
* **GPS module** (powered from ESP32 3.3 V output)

### Wiring Steps

1. **Battery to TP4056**

   * Battery **+** → TP4056 **B+**
   * Battery **–** → TP4056 **B-**

2. **TP4056 Output to Switch**

   * TP4056 **OUT+** → Switch input
   * TP4056 **OUT–** → Boost converter GND

3. **Switch to Boost Converter**

   * Switch output → Boost converter **VIN+**
   * Boost converter GND already tied to **OUT–**

4. **Boost Converter to ESP32**

   * Boost converter **VOUT+ (5 V)** → ESP32 **5V** pin
   * Boost converter GND → ESP32 **GND**

5. **GPS Module**

   * Connect to ESP32 3.3 V pin and GND as usual.

### Operation

* **Charging**: Plug micro‑USB into TP4056. Battery charges while the system can remain ON or OFF.
* **Power ON/OFF**: Slider switch isolates the boost converter, fully cutting power to ESP32 when OFF.

### Notes

* Adjust boost converter to **5.0 V output** before connecting ESP32.
* TP4056 with protection prevents over‑charge/over‑discharge.
* Ensure battery capacity is adequate for GPS + ESP32 current draw (~200‑400 mA typical).
